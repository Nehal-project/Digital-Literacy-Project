# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and first-time digital payment users. It involves tricking individuals into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details.

## How the Fraud Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The attacker contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or social media pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer.
2. They create urgency (e.g., "Your account will be blocked" or "You will receive a refund").
3. The victim is asked to click on a malicious link or scan a QR code.
4. The victim is then prompted to enter UPI PIN or approve a payment request.
5. Once approved, money is instantly transferred to the fraudster's account.

## Target Audience
- College students using UPI apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm
- People unfamiliar with digital payment security
- Individuals responding quickly without verifying sources

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss
- Difficulty recovering money
- Emotional stress and panic
- Risk of further scams if data is leaked

## Example Scenario
A student receives a call claiming they won a cashback reward. They are asked to scan a QR code to "receive" money. Instead, they unknowingly approve a payment and lose ₹5,000.

## Conclusion
UPI fraud relies more on psychological manipulation than technical hacking. Awareness and caution are the best defenses.
