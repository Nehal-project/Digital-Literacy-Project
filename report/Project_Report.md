## Introduction

This project was assigned as part of the Digital Literacy course, where I took on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador. The objective was to build a Digital Literacy Portfolio to help students understand essential digital skills required in today’s world.

The project consisted of five tasks: creating a digital literacy infographic, building a professional digital portfolio, exploring coding and collaboration platforms, understanding email etiquette, and studying cyber crime awareness. Each task helped develop practical skills such as online communication, safe internet usage, and professional digital presence.

Overall, this project provided hands-on exposure to important digital tools and concepts that are useful for both academic and professional growth.

---

## Task 1 — Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

**Tool Used:** Canva

**What My Design Shows:**

My infographic presents a structured overview of digital literacy for students. It includes three main sections: understanding digital literacy, safe internet practices, and email etiquette for students.The first section explains how digital literacy involves accessing, evaluating, and creating information online. The second section highlights on online safety, including strong passwords, two-factor authentication, phishing awareness, and protecting personal information. The third section focuses on email etiquette like using clear subject lines, staying concise, and proofreading to ensure respectful, polished, and efficient workplace communication .
**Reflection:**

One thing I found interesting was how visual design can make information easier to understand compared to plain text. However, I found it slightly difficult to balance content and design, as adding too much text made the infographic look crowded. This task helped me improve both my understanding of digital literacy and my ability to present information effectively.

## Task 4 — Professional Email & Etiquette Guide

**Communication Scenario:**

A student sends an informal email to a professor without a proper subject line, greeting, or clear message. As a result, the email is ignored or misunderstood, causing delay in response and confusion.

**Key Learnings:**

Professional communication requires clarity, proper structure, and respectful tone. Using a clear subject line, formal greeting, and well-structured message can improve communication effectiveness. This task helped me understand how to write professional emails and behave responsibly online.

## Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness Case Study

**Cybercrime Type Researched:** UPI Fraud

**What Surprised You Most:**

I was surprised to learn how easily people fall victim to UPI fraud due to lack of awareness. Many scams rely more on psychological tricks than technical hacking.

**One Habit You Will Personally Change:**

I will always verify payment requests carefully and never enter my UPI PIN unless I am making a payment. I will also avoid clicking on unknown links and be more cautious during online transactions.
