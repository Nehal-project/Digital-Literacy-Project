# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist

## For Responsible Social Media Use as a College Student

---

## ✅ Do's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Keep your profile professional (clear photo, real name, proper bio) | Recruiters and professors may check your profile |
| 2 | Think before posting anything | Online content is permanent and can be shared |
| 3 | Verify information before sharing | Prevents spreading fake news or misinformation |
| 4 | Use strong, unique passwords | Protects accounts from hacking |
| 5 | Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) | Adds extra layer of security |
| 6 | Respect others’ privacy | Builds trust and avoids conflicts |
| 7 | Engage in meaningful discussions | Helps build a positive digital reputation |
| 8 | Limit screen time and distractions | Improves productivity and focus |
| 9 | Customize privacy settings regularly | Controls who sees your content |
| 10 | Build a professional presence (LinkedIn, GitHub) | Helps in internships and career growth |

---

## ❌ Don'ts

| # | Don't | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Do not share personal info (phone, address, ID) | Can lead to identity theft |
| 2 | Do not cyberbully or troll | Legal consequences + harms others |
| 3 | Do not accept unknown friend requests | Many accounts are fake/scammers |
| 4 | Do not post when emotional or angry | Can damage reputation |
| 5 | Do not share fake giveaways or suspicious links | Often phishing scams |
| 6 | Do not plagiarize content | Violates ethics and copyright |
| 7 | Do not overshare daily activities/location | Increases safety risks |
| 8 | Do not click unknown links | Can lead to hacking or malware |
| 9 | Do not ignore suspicious messages | Early action prevents damage |
| 10 | Do not waste excessive time on social media | Affects academics and mental health |

---

## 🔒 Key Rule
> If you wouldn’t say it in front of your professor or future employer, don’t post it online.

---

## 📌 Final Thought
Your digital footprint defines your personal brand. Use social media to grow, not harm your future.
